                  Cluster 1 — I Need to Be a Better Student for College
                 Interviewees: Jose, Jeremy
                 JTBD statement
                 When I am starting college and realizing my high school habits will not cut it anymore, I want
                 to finally become the organized, prepared student I have always told myself I would be, so I
                 do not maintain complacency and fall behind in a new environment.
                 Anchor quote
                 “I thought to myself, like, I’m going to be locked in, I’m going to be focused in college.” —
                 Jose

                 4 Forces
                 Push
                       College material is harder and the workload less predictable, with no built-in structure to
                       keep them on track.
                       High school autopilot — coasting and relying on memory — is no longer a viable strategy.

                 Interviewees: Advik, Caleb
                 JTBD statement
                 When a preventable academic failure or unproductive work session shows me that my
                 reactive habits are costing me, I want a system that makes important work visible earlier, so I
                 can stop cramming, use my time better, and feel back in control.
                 Anchor quote
                 “I felt like I messed it up myself and it was completely avoidable.” — Advik

                 4 Forces
                 Push
                       Poor exam performance that felt avoidable. Advik: “I felt like I messed it up myself and it
                       was completely avoidable.”
                       Unproductive sessions despite having time and energy. Caleb: “I felt like I wasn’t being
                       that productive studying in Mudd, even though I had things to do.”
                       Procrastination cycles compounding into deadline crunch. Caleb: “Those hours wasted —
                       even though I don’t feel them now, two weeks after I will feel them and I will have to
                       crunch.”
                 Pull
                       Everything visible in one place before it becomes urgent.
                       Targeted reminders that actually prompt action. Caleb: “The reminders — not the useless
                       reminders. For this it was more targeted, to the point where I actually went back and
                       actually did something about it.”
                       Structure that reduces mental load and creates the feeling of being on the right track.
                 Habit
                       Canvas plus memory plus last-minute cramming.
                       Switching to easier tasks when stuck.
                       Motivation talks from friends or family in place of a real system.
                       Reactive, urgency-driven workflow.
                 Anxiety
                       Doubt about staying disciplined enough.
                       Paying for something then abandoning it.
