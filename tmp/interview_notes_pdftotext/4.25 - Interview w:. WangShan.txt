             Timeline
             First Thought — Middle school. WangShan first started thinking about time management using
             paper notebooks. The instinct that her time/tasks needed structure was there from very early on,
             well before any app.
             Passive Looking — Second year of college, around 2016. Workload picked up: lots of courses
             and homework, plus juggling a course-schedule app and Apple Notes/memos for birthdays and
             miscellaneous tasks. She noticed Apple Notes was getting visually disorganized but kept using it
             out of inertia — too many existing records to walk away from. Idea of "a better system" was
             floating around, but no real search yet.
             Event #1 — Forgot a friend's birthday — Missed a friend's birthday and didn't buy a gift. That
             single failure crystallized the problem: her scattered setup (course app + Notes + memos) wasn't
             actually holding her life together. This is what flipped her from passively noticing to actively
             looking for a comprehensive life-management tool.
             Active Looking (2016–2023) — A long, intermittent search.
                       2016–2017: Tried Di Da Clock (滴答时钟) and other apps friends recommended. Each one
                       was either too complicated or too single-function. As a college student with no budget for
                       paid apps, she defaulted back to Apple Notes + Calendar.
                       2017–2023: Kept periodically looking but never found anything satisfying. Started
                       working, initially used work apps for time management. Began watching efficiency/self-
                       growth creators on YouTube and Bilibili, which kept the search alive in the background.
             Event #2 — Bilibili creator's life-story video (2023) — While still arguably in active looking, she
             saw a video where a creator shared her personal life story and mentioned using 指尖时光 to
             manage her own time. The combination of a trusted creator + a concrete app name + a real use
             case (personal time, not just work) is what flipped her from "still looking" to "go search this on
             Bilibili now." By this point she also had income, so paying was no longer a blocker.
             Deciding — Searched Bilibili for time-management tools, compared several apps recommended
             in that creator's circle. Picked 指尖时光 because it was the only one that bundled check-in,
             scheduling, to-do list, and birthday/memorial-day tracking in a single app — directly solving the
             original "forgot a friend's birthday" problem and the long-standing "too many separate apps"
             problem.
             Buying — Started with the free trial, but it was too limited. Within a few days she purchased the
             full membership because it met all her needs.
             Consuming — Still actively uses the app, mainly the calendar and Daka functions.
