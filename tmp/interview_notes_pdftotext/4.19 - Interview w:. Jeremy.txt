             Timeline
             First thought — Early September (~Sept 5), still at home. Saw a YouTube Shorts/TikTok creator's
             "what you need in your dorm" video — mini fridge, outlets, planner. The whiteboard planner idea
             lands as a background "that seems cool" thought. Not the exact model he ended up buying, but
             the same category.
             Passive looking — Kept consuming general startup/college-life content and noticed study
             strategies floating around (schedule your college life, how to be a better student). Thought about
             planners as a "nice addition," never acted on it. No search, no list.
             Event 1 — the suitcase situation — Out-of-state freshman, couldn't fit everything in suitcases.
             Stayed at a Skokie hotel during Wildcat Welcome (the closer Evanston hotel was already
             booked). Target was right next door. This is what actually forced him into shopping mode.
             Active looking — At Target with his mom, working down the aisles. Necessities were priority #1;
             "supplementary stuff to help me be a better student" was a soft priority #2 if time allowed. The
             planner wasn't on a written list — just a vague thought in the back of his head.
             Event 2 — stumbling across the aisle — Walked past the whiteboard planner in Target. It
             triggered recall of the video. He didn’t decide between buying or not buying — he immediately
             started choosing which one.
             Deciding — Compared different whiteboard planners with different colored covers. Picked the
             whiteboard because: doesn't have to be carried (sticks to the wall), dry-erase and reusable,
             colored markers let him distinguish exam dates vs. events vs. notes, has calendar slots plus a
             notes section, and it looked bulky/well-stocked which he read as a quality signal.
             Buying — Straightforward. Bought it at Target in Skokie before school started.
             Consuming — after the math test — Sat unused on his desk for weeks. The Math 230-1
             (differential/multivariable calc) first midterm is what actually pulled it into use: under-prepared,
             didn't know exam dates, bad score, "unsatisfactory, I don't like this." That's when he started
             writing exam dates, due dates, and reminders on it, right in front of his face at his desk.
             Satisfaction — yes — Second midterm: way better prepared. Clear upward trend from that point,
             and he credits the planner for putting him in a proactive position. Pivoted away from it today for
             digital solutions, but was satisfied through the duration of usage.
